About Kev

Kevin Matthew Penn was 48 years old when he passed away from a 2-year battle with melanoma. Born October 1, 1971, in Camp Hill, PA, he was the son of G. Frank and Kathleen M. (Sullivan) Penn and was a 1990 graduate of West Perry High School, Elliottsburg, PA.

Following high school, Kevin received his Bachelor’s Degree in Mathematics from Millersville University and his Master’s Degree in Mathematics Education from the City University of New York.

Kevin was a high school math teacher, most recently from Edward A. Reynolds West Side High School in Manhattan, where he was a perennial student favorite.

Upon learning of Kevin’s passing, a fellow teacher posted a photo online of Kevin hugging a crying student at graduation, with the following caption, “the day Kevin never gave up on a student and she graduated from high school, at 22 years old and while working in healthcare. She was a hard girl but cried with happiness because the only person to keep faith in her was Kevin.” With West Side Pride, Kevin also coached rugby and softball for West Side High School.

The Foundation

Award:

The Kevin M. Penn Memorial Foundation’s fundamental purpose ignites the power of community with education by providing annual postsecondary scholarships to graduating students facing financial hardship.

This one-time scholarship worth $1000 is meant to encourage postsecondary education and support students in the community in which Kevin taught.

  • $1000 per recipient
  • 1 recipient per school calendar year
  • Application Deadline: 1st Monday in June to Edward A. Reynolds School Counseling Office or electronically at kevinmpennfoundation@gmail.com
  • Award announced at June graduation
Eligibility:
  • Student must be enrolling in postsecondary education/training
  • Must be a High School student in current graduating class at Edwards A. Reynolds, West Side High School
