Foundation Provider

The Kevin M. Penn Memorial Foundation was created with love by some of Kevin’s closest friends and family to honor his spirit. Kevin was diagnosed with advanced stage melanoma in April 2018 and fought a courageous and hard battle with this devastating disease until he passed away on April 24, 2020.

Kevin was a special person who impacted the lives of all who knew him. He was a gifted math teacher and a beloved colleague at Edward A. Reynolds West Side High School. He always went above and beyond to support his students and the entire West Side Community.

Kevin had a patient and effective way of teaching math to all of his students. He also connected with students outside of the classroom by sharing his love of physical activity. He coached the rugby team, the softball team and he led the after school walking club. Kevin would often inspire students and staff to join him on runs, bike rides and workouts before and after school.

He believed all young adults can attain postsecondary education regardless of personal hardships while shaping a balanced and unforgettable life. It is in the spirit that the Foundation will continue Kevin’s legacy, giving hard-working, loving, free-spirited kids who have faced hardship a second chance.

The Bigsy Award

annual scholarship will provide funding for students who share many of Kevin’s qualities and who have proven their discipline to overcome hardships and shape a balanced and unforgettable life for them and others around them.

He was beautiful inside and out, he was generous, he was brilliant, he was humble, and he was kind. These qualities have inspired us to create this scholarship fund to keep his spirit alive by helping the students in the community he taught.

  • $1000 per recipient
  • 1 recipient per school calendar year
  • Application Deadline: 1st Monday in June to Edward A. Reynolds School Counseling Office or electronically at kevinmpennfoundation@gmail.com
  • Award announced at June graduation
Eligibility:
  • Student must be enrolling in postsecondary education/training
  • Must be a High School student in current graduating class at Edwards A. Reynolds, West Side High School
